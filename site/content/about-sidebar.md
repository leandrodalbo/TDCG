## About

This site documents the day-to-day experience of building production-ready software by combining AI-assisted development with established software engineering practices.

The interesting part is how requirements are defined, how tests drive development, how architecture evolves, how code is reviewed, and how practices such as TDD, SOLID, KISS, YAGNI, and DRY adapt to this way of working.

The articles here are drawn from real work. They capture decisions, trade-offs, mistakes, refactorings, and the reasoning behind them. Some posts focus on specific implementation details. Others examine the process itself and the methods emerging from the combination of traditional engineering discipline and AI-assisted development.

Think of it as a journal rather than a collection of tutorials: a record of what was built, why it was built that way, and what was learned along the way.
